# Limitations

MESSINA is a comparative stress-testing framework, not a forecasting engine or total simulation of reality. Its usefulness depends on disciplined interpretation as much as scenario construction.

## 1. MESSINA is not a forecasting system

MESSINA does not forecast real-world probabilities. It does not claim to predict whether a specific event will occur, when it will occur, or at what exact intensity.

## 2. Comparative proxies, not direct measurements

Indicators such as decision friction, governance erosion, platform dependency, and mosaic response are comparative proxies. Their main value lies in directional comparison across documented conditions.

## 3. Scenario results depend on input construction

If opening shocks, actor assumptions, corridor constraints, or governance overlays are poorly framed, outputs will reflect those weaknesses.

## 4. Stronger on coupled-system stress than tactical combat realism

MESSINA is stronger at representing shipping disruption, insurance repricing, fertilizer and food-input stress, sovereign-risk transmission, alliance hesitation, credibility erosion, governance friction decay, and second-order systemic effects. It is weaker at representing detailed targeting dynamics, tactical air and naval realism, exact attrition rates, or fine-grained real-time command degradation.

## 5. Actor behavior is stylized

The framework incorporates mechanisms informed by organizational delay, prospect-style loss sensitivity, rally effects, sunk-cost escalation, coercive diplomacy, complex interdependence, and hegemonic-erosion logic. These are structured assumptions, not complete psychological or institutional models.

## 6. Public reproducibility is bounded

This repository supports scrutiny of the documented v25.9 governance-observer surface. It does not expose the full historical internal engine, every older scenario family, or every planned future layer.

## 7. Non-public module engine boundary

The broader architecture includes two dozen internal module families. The current public repository does not execute all of them. The module list is an architectural inventory and future public-release target. The public reproducibility claim rests on the observer-only governance formulas, configs, artifacts, and runner.

## 8. Design priors and confirmation risk

Several weights are design priors rather than empirical estimates. If used carelessly, the model can become a structured expression of the author's assumptions. The defense is transparency, not neutrality by assertion: formulas, configs, assumptions, and overlays must be visible enough for readers to challenge, alter, and rerun the comparison.

## 9. Institutional friction is not always good

The framework does not claim that more friction is always better. Delay can help adversaries adapt, disperse, or exploit hesitation. The claim is narrower: too little friction can degrade control over second-order effects in tightly coupled crises.

## 10. Results are scenario-relative

Hormuz-centered outputs should not be transferred mechanically to Bab el-Mandeb, Suez, Panama, Taiwan / South China Sea, or other chokepoint environments without topology-specific assumptions.

## 11. Monte Carlo repetition does not create external probability truth

The broader methodology lineage includes bounded Monte Carlo use, including MC512. That does not mean the current public build is a rare-event frequency engine or a source of real-world probabilities.
