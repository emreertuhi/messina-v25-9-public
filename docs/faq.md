# FAQ
## Frequently Asked Questions about MESSINA

## 1. Is MESSINA a forecasting model?
No. MESSINA is not a forecasting engine. It does not assign reliable real-world probabilities or tell the user exactly what will happen next.

## 2. What does it do?
It compares how a structured crisis world behaves under explicit conditions. It is built to show how crises propagate, how pathways diverge, and how visible stress and control quality can move differently.

## 3. Is MESSINA a theory-testing model?
No, not in the strong empirical sense. It is a mechanism-encoding framework informed by theory and relevant literatures.

## 4. What is the public theory hierarchy?
Public-facing theory framing remains narrow:
- primary: securitization
- secondary: normal accidents
- related coercive logic: Pape-informed findings

Broader design memory exists, but it is not the same thing as the public theory identity of the framework.

## 5. Why is the model layered?
Because crises do not look the same at every scale. Framing pressure, behavior, institutions, propagation, and regime change do not live comfortably at one resolution.

## 6. What is a scenario pack?
A scenario pack is the case-specific layer that defines chronology, priors, vulnerabilities, variants, and starting conditions. The scenario pack is not the same thing as the engine.

## 7. Why does scenario-pack dependence matter?
Because it prevents overclaim. A result produced under one encoded case world should not be casually generalized into a universal law.

## 8. Are the numbers direct measurements?
No. The main quantities in this repository are normalized indices, comparative proxies, composite summaries, and structured outputs.

## 9. What is the difference between visible stress and control quality?
Visible stress refers to how much crisis pressure is present in the system. Control quality refers to whether the system can still process contradiction, coordinate response, and govern second-order effects. These can diverge.

## 10. What does v25.9 add?
v25.9 adds observer-side governance diagnostics and a bounded same-shock comparison surface. It does not claim a full causal governance engine.

## 11. Why does the repository care so much about wording?
Because wording changes the burden of proof. Safer language protects the repository from claiming more than the current build can support.

## 12. What is the safest description of MESSINA?
> MESSINA is a mechanism-encoding, research-grade scenario-exploration framework whose outputs should be read as comparative stress diagnostics under encoded assumptions.


## 13. Does Monte Carlo repetition make the outputs probabilities?
No. Repetition improves comparative stability and branch reading, but it does not convert internal outputs into externally validated real-world probabilities.

## 14. Are modules the same thing as islands?
No. Islands, where used, are Mezzo-level aggregates or semi-cohesive actor groupings. Modules are functional mechanism bundles such as governance diagnostics, corridor logic, or strategic-buyer logic.

## 15. Why does the repository mention ODYSSEUS?
ODYSSEUS Scenario Pack v1.0 is part of the publication-safe lineage of the Hormuz case. In the current public release it appears only as historical lineage context, not as a fully released public scenario pack.


## 16. Does the current public release include a friction spectrum?
Yes, in bounded config form. The public package now includes mild, moderate, and severe reduced-friction overlays between the documented high-friction baseline and the frictionless counterfactual.

## 17. Do the policy overlays directly simulate law?
No. They are illustrative governance-condition overlays built on the public-safe runner. They help narrow the gap between policy interpretation and executable public comparison, but they are not direct legal simulation.

## 18. Does the public package include scenario breadth beyond Hormuz?
It now includes non-executable public briefs for additional chokepoints and theaters, but the executable runner remains centered on the Hormuz-style v25.9 public case surface.
