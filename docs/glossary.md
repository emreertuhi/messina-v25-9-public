# Glossary
## MESSINA Repository Terms

### Aggregation
The procedures that convert raw simulation histories into interpretable summaries, pathway descriptions, regime labels, comparative outputs, and validation artifacts. In MESSINA, aggregation is part of the interpretive architecture rather than a reporting afterthought.

### Branch dominance
A recurrent tendency for one crisis pathway or regime pathway to appear more strongly than others under repeated runs of the same encoded scenario family.

### Braking architecture
The institutional, procedural, legal, and political structures that slow, test, qualify, or contest executive momentum before it hardens into systemic exposure.

### Circulation crisis
A crisis in which a local shock propagates through the systems that keep trade, pricing, supply, coordination, and political confidence functioning.

### Comparative proxy
A model construct designed to support disciplined comparison across runs, overlays, or scenarios.

### Complex-systems aware
A description for frameworks that already encode non-linearity, heterogeneity, feedback, thresholds, path dependence, and cascade structure even if they do not yet implement every possible diagnostic layer.

### Control quality
The capacity of a political-institutional system to contain, process, absorb, and steer crisis consequences.

### Engine
The reusable mechanism framework that processes a scenario world.

### Fractal reading
A design-memory term referring to the intuition that fragmentation, synchronization, and collapse-relevant structures may recur across micro, mezzo, network, and macro levels. It does **not** imply that the current public build is already a strict mathematical fractal model.

### Friction
Institutional, procedural, legal, or political constraints that slow or qualify action.

### Governance erosion
A comparative indicator of declining institutional capacity to govern crisis consequences.

### Mixed-resolution model
A model that does not treat all layers or actors at the same level of abstraction.

### Multiscale
A framework property indicating that crisis structure is represented across multiple interacting levels rather than collapsed into one aggregate space.

### Observer layer
A diagnostic or interpretive layer that reads model behavior without altering engine dynamics.

### Path aggregation
Aggregation focused on how runs arrive at outcomes over time.

### Platform dependency
Reliance on commercial routing, data, monitoring, or coordination infrastructures that the state uses but does not fully control.

### Provenance
Metadata that records where an assumption came from, why it was used, how confident the modeler is in it, and whether it is calibrated, stylized, or provisional.

### Regime aggregation
Aggregation focused on classifying runs into behaviorally meaningful types rather than only reporting terminal scores.

### Scenario pack
The case-specific layer that defines chronology, priors, vulnerabilities, variants, and starting conditions.

### Stress-testing framework
A framework that explores how structures behave under shocks and perturbations rather than claiming to predict exact real-world outcomes.


### Island (internal term)
A semi-cohesive actor aggregate formed at the Mezzo layer, where multiple micro-level actors or behavior clusters are grouped into a bounded institutional, sectoral, logistical, or political unit.

An island is defined by stronger internal coupling than external linkage. In later stages of the framework, islands may be represented as nodes or clustered subnetworks in the Network layer.

The term is internal and interpretive. It should not be confused with:
- a literal geographic island
- a fully isolated system
- a functional module

### MC512
A historically important publication-level Monte Carlo ensemble size used for branch stability, phase consistency, and scenario comparison.

MC512 should be defended narrowly:
- useful for branch and phase stability
- not sufficient for strong rare-event frequency precision claims

### Nano
The smallest analytically meaningful layer in the framework, used for bounded local events, trigger conditions, signal injections, and discrete shock elements before they are aggregated upward.

### ODYSSEUS Scenario Pack v1.0
The historically important publication-safe Hormuz case layer in the earlier MESSINA framing. In the current public repository it should be treated as lineage context rather than as a fully released public scenario pack.


### Scenario brief
A non-executable public document used to mark future scenario breadth, preserve mechanism distinctions, or clarify portability limits without claiming that the current runner already executes the case.
