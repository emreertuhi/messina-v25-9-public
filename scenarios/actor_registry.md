# Actor Registry
## Public Actor Families in MESSINA

This file records the main actors and actor families relevant to the current public scenario surface.

## Core public actor families

### 1. Coercive bloc
A strategic coercive actor set or bloc that initiates or sustains chokepoint pressure.

### 2. Retaliatory maritime actor set
A distributed responder whose actions can push pressure sideways into circulation channels such as shipping, insurance, and routing.

### 3. Strategic buyers
External buyer classes whose behavior can become a test of enforcement credibility, burden-sharing, and routing stability.

### 4. Insurer and shipping actor families
Commercial actors that can reprice, reroute, or narrow acceptable risk faster than states can reimpose coherence.

### 5. Allied or partner actors
Actors whose consultation, hesitation, qualification, or legal caution can function as friction, disunity, or both.

### 6. Platform and infrastructure intermediaries
Commercial or technical systems that states rely on for routing, data, coordination, or monitoring without fully controlling them.

## Reading rule
Actors in MESSINA are role-bearing units inside the scenario logic. They are not always literal individuals or perfectly unified sovereign actors.
