# Bab el-Mandeb Scenario Brief

## Status
Non-executable public scenario brief.

## Why it matters
Bab el-Mandeb is a chokepoint where intermittent disruption can propagate through Red Sea routing, insurance, carrier scheduling, food-input timing, and coalition burden-sharing without matching a Hormuz-style energy shock exactly.

## Public-use purpose
This brief exists to widen scenario breadth without pretending that the current public runner already operationalizes a full Bab el-Mandeb pack.

## Distinctive structural features
- stronger rerouting relevance through Red Sea / Suez-linked traffic
- intermittent rather than absolute closure logic
- shipping and insurance sensitivity may dominate before energy-price narratives do
- coalition naval burden-sharing is likely to matter differently than in a Hormuz-centered case

## Reading rule
Use this brief as a future comparison target and mechanism cue, not as a claim that current v25.9 public outputs already generalize to Bab el-Mandeb.
