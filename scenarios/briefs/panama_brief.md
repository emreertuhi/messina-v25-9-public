# Panama Scenario Brief

## Status
Non-executable public scenario brief.

## Why it matters
Panama matters less for immediate coercive naval confrontation than for infrastructure bottleneck logic, rerouting cost, time loss, and commercial dependency under constrained transit capacity.

## Public-use purpose
This brief widens the framework's visible breadth by marking a different chokepoint family: capacity-constrained passage with strong scheduling, cost, and routing consequences.

## Distinctive structural features
- infrastructure and queue dynamics over direct military coercion
- stronger timing and logistics sensitivity
- different alliance and deterrence structure than Hormuz
- possible interaction with food, commodity, and supply-chain timing rather than direct war-risk repricing alone

## Reading rule
Panama should be treated as a separate chokepoint family requiring different scenario encoding before executable comparison becomes credible.
