# Assumption Ledger
## Public Scenario Assumptions in MESSINA

This file records the major assumptions that shape the current public scenario surface.

## Core rule

> MESSINA outputs are meaningful under encoded assumptions, not as assumption-free laws of the real world.

## Confirmed public assumptions

### 1. Same shock, different governance conditions
The public comparison holds the chokepoint crisis frame broadly constant while varying governance conditions.

### 2. Observer-only governance logic does not rewrite core peak-stress dynamics
The v25.9 governance layer is diagnostic rather than a full new causal sovereignty engine.

### 3. Platform dependency may be scenario-configured
Platform dependency in the public release can be partly supplied by scenario configuration rather than fully emerging from a deep empirical submodel.

### 4. Reduced-friction conditions degrade governability more sharply
The documented comparison surface encodes the claim that reduced friction can worsen governance diagnostics while leaving visible stress broadly similar.

### 5. Scenario outputs remain case-dependent
The current public outputs belong first to the encoded case world and comparison setup rather than to all chokepoint crises in general.

## Reading rule
This ledger does not prove that the assumptions are true in reality. It makes the comparison surface inspectable and prevents hidden assumptions from pretending to be facts.
